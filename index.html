Field App Help

1. Gather Location

Use Gather Location to capture a control, waypoint, or reference point.
Enter the location details, select the Location type and No., and add any notes required.
To capture GPS coordinates, place the mobile device about 1 m off the ground with a clear view of the sky. Avoid obstructions above approximately 30 degrees from the horizon.
Select Gather Location, then walk away from the device. When the capture is complete, an alarm will sound. Check the quality rating before saving or continuing.
If known coordinates are available, they can optionally be entered using Manual Entry.
After the point has been captured, prepare the trip line for Test Location. Select Walk / Drive, wait for the settle period to complete, then walk or drive along the road for approximately 20 to 30 m before and after the captured point. The trip line coordinates will be presented.
Save the location when complete. The trip line can also be added later using the Rally Mapping application.

2. Test Location

Use Test Location to test a saved point or trip line for vehicle detection.
When at, or passing, the point or line, press the manual button. The app will present the recorded times.
At walking pace, a difference of a second or so can translate to sub-second differences at driving speeds. If the error is greater than expected, review the accuracy of the captured point or trip line.
Watch the distance to line while conducting the test.

3. GPS Trip

Use GPS Trip to record GPS distance while driving.
Pause the recording when stopping to gather or test a location.
Use Mark Spot to note a distance or location while moving. Notes can be added, and the data can be shared later.

4. Route Validation

Use Route Validation to compare the mapped route distance with the GPS distance recorded while driving.
Load the required route and select the waypoint where validation will begin.
Start Trip Now is used for a standing start at the selected waypoint.
Arm Start at Waypoint is used for a moving start. The trip begins automatically when the selected waypoint's trip line or 30 m radius is detected.
As each waypoint is detected, its card shows the mapped distance, GPS distance and difference.
The quality letter shows the GPS accuracy used for the result: G — Good, green, up to 8 m; F — Fair, orange, over 8 m and up to 15 m; P — Poor, red, worse than 15 m; and B — Bad, red, bad or failed detection.
The optional green Time button is available within 300 m before or after the active waypoint. Hold it for 500 ms; the phone timer records the initial touch time and the card shows the automatic and button elapsed-time difference. If it is not used, the card remains distance-only.
Stop and Review ends the current validation run and opens the review/reset controls.
Note: Small differences between the mapped distance and the GPS odometer distance are normal. The mapped route follows the map routing line, while the vehicle may travel a slightly different line through bends, lanes and junctions.
If the accumulated Diff becomes large enough that it may hide later local differences, select Stop and Review, then restart validation from the last correctly recorded waypoint to create a fresh baseline.
As a guide, consider restarting when the accumulated Diff reaches about 50 m.
Getting a route file into the app:
Save the required Rally Mapping route file and email it to your mobile device. Open the email and select the attachment's Download icon. Then open the Rally Field App, select Load Route to Validate, and select the required downloaded route file.

5. Export Data

At the end of the session, use Download Saved Data.
Open the downloaded file and share it by WhatsApp, email, or Drive for import into the Rally Mapping application.
